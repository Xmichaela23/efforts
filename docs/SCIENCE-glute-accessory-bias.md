# SCIENCE — Glute Accessory Bias (Get Stronger add-on)

Grounding for the **glute accessory-bias** add-on — an optional posterior-chain slot on the Get Stronger chassis (§2a of `ROADMAP-hybrid-strength-addons.md`), sharing one mechanism with the Hyrox layer. **House style:** every line tagged **CITED** (peer-reviewed, checked against the primary this session — use exactly), **CONVENTION** (practitioner consensus, not a peer-reviewed constant), or **UNVERIFIED/MIXED** (evidence weak or does not support the strong version). Extracted + re-verified from the vaguer glute references in `STRENGTH-PROTOCOL.md` (which cited "Contreras et al." / "Distefano et al." with no locators).

**The honesty line, first:** direct glute work builds the glute — that's SOUND. Whether it makes you *run faster* is **NOT proven** (the transfer RCT was null). So the athlete-facing pitch is **stronger, more durable hips**, not a speed promise. Say it that way.

---

## 1. What it IS + what it's FOR (role)

An optional **posterior-chain accessory slot** (hip thrust / single-leg / back extension) on the Get Stronger split — hypertrophy dose (8–12), glute-specific selection. The main lifts, ATR arc, %s, deload, and retest are **untouched**; this is one added accessory, not a program change. Aesthetic goal is **unspoken**; the copy leads with a durability/performance rationale, honestly bounded (§4).

## 2. Cited principles (verified against primaries, 2026-07-01)

- **CITED — the hip thrust is the most glute-*specific* loaded exercise; ~2× the glute EMG of the squat.** Mean upper-glute-max activation 69.5% vs 29.4% MVIC (≈90–140% greater across regions) — the "~75%" often quoted is a garbled secondary figure; the paper's numbers are larger.
  - **Contreras B, Vigotsky AD, Schoenfeld BJ, Beardsley C, Cronin J (2015).** *A Comparison of Gluteus Maximus, Biceps Femoris, and Vastus Lateralis EMG Activity in the Back Squat and Barbell Hip Thrust Exercises.* J Appl Biomech **31(6):452–458.**
- **CITED — hip thrust is glute-SPECIFIC, not glute-SUPERIOR for growth.** MRI, volume-equated, 9 wk: gluteus-maximus hypertrophy was **similar** between back squat and hip thrust (the squat grew the thighs more). So the honest pitch is *targets the glute specifically, on top of what the squat already gives* — power/strength without unnecessary thigh mass, which is what an endurance athlete wants.
  - **Plotkin DL, … Contreras BM, et al. (2023).** *Hip thrust and back squat training elicit similar gluteus muscle hypertrophy and transfer similarly to the deadlift.* Front Physiol **14:1279170.** PMID 37461495.
- **CITED — single-leg work maximally activates the glutes; grounds the single-leg selection.** EMG across 12 therapeutic exercises: **single-limb squat and single-limb deadlift** produced the highest gluteus-*maximus* activation; **side-lying hip abduction** the highest gluteus-*medius*. (Measures activation/recruitment, not injury outcomes — phrase as "maximally recruits," not "proven to prevent injury.")
  - **DiStefano LJ, Blackburn JT, Marshall SW, Padua DA (2009).** *Gluteal muscle activation during common therapeutic exercises.* J Orthop Sports Phys Ther **39(7):532–540.** doi:10.2519/jospt.2009.2796. PMID 19574661.
- **CITED — the interference basis for placement:** concurrent interference is real, dose-dependent, and **running interferes more than cycling** — the reason the bias slot avoids heavy-Lower / long-run adjacency (§3).
  - **Wilson JM, et al. (2012).** *Concurrent training: a meta-analysis…* J Strength Cond Res **26(8):2293–2307.** PMID 22002517.

## 3. Interference-aware placement (shared with the Hyrox layer)

Running is **eccentric-impact-dominant** — each ground contact drives large eccentric loads into quads/hams/glutes (well-established; strongest in downhill running [Vernillo 2025 review, *Front Bioeng Biotechnol*] — **CONVENTION-strength background**). Heavy Lower loads the same tissue. So the bias slot must NOT stack posterior-chain eccentric volume on a heavy-Lower day or a day adjacent to the long run.

**The engine call:** the single bias slot lands on **Upper A** — an upper day (no heavy-Lower conflict) and the earliest in the week, maximally removed from the weekend long run. This satisfies the constraint for *any* selection (even the eccentric ones, walking lunge / back extension). **⚠ FLAG FOR MICHAEL:** the alternative is a lower-day placement restricted to low-eccentric selection (hip thrust is concentric-dominant, so it could sit on the heavy-Lower day as the tri protocol's S-005 already does). The engine ships the conservative Upper-A call; this is a training-science preference worth a human read.

## 4. What is CONVENTION / MIXED — flag honestly (the copy depends on this)

- **MIXED / NOT PROVEN — "stronger glutes make you run faster" (training transfer).** The one RCT was **NULL**: six weeks of hip thrust vs front squat showed **no clear sprint or horizontal-jump benefit** for the hip thrust group [**Contreras BM, et al. (2017).** *Effects of a Six-Week Hip Thrust vs. Front Squat…* J Strength Cond Res **31(4):999–1008**]. Support for "hip extension → propulsion" is **correlational only** (hip-thrust power associates with 0–10 m acceleration). → **Do NOT promise faster running.** The honest copy is *stronger, more durable hips*; hip-extension power *relates to* acceleration, transfer is unproven.
- **CONVENTION — "cyclists under-recruit their glutes."** No primary supports the "under-recruitment vs potential" framing. The citable fact is only that the **glute max is a relatively low-activation contributor in cycling** vs the quads/calf, rising with workload [**Ericson MO, et al. (1985).** *Muscular activity during ergometer cycling.* Scand J Rehabil Med **17(2):53–61.** PMID 4023660]. → Frame as "the pedal stroke doesn't fully load the glutes; direct work builds that hip-extension capacity" — NOT "you're failing to use them."
- **CONVENTION — the exact hypertrophy dose (8–12, RIR 2–3) and the rotation** sit on top of the cited selection science; calibrated by the athlete.

## 6. The microcopy (honest wording the engine ships)

- **Runner:** *"Direct hip-extension + single-leg glute work — builds the hip strength and stability that hold up over long mileage."* (durability, not a speed promise)
- **Cyclist:** *"The pedal stroke leaves the glutes under-loaded — direct hip-extension work builds the power the saddle doesn't."* (capacity, not "you're doing it wrong")
