# SCIENCE — Upkeep / Maintenance (the POV behind the upkeep nudge)

**2026-07-17.** The science-backed, discipline-agnostic POV for when a user sets a discipline to **upkeep (maintain)** and it slips below target. Seeds the future glass-box "science" section (full transparency — every claim carries a receipt). Discipline-agnostic on purpose: the user may put run, bike, swim, or strength on upkeep — the copy fills from ONE model, never a running template with the word swapped.

## The one principle

Aerobic fitness has two parts, and they behave differently when a discipline drops:

- **Central (the engine)** — heart, stroke volume, blood volume, VO2max. *Systemic.* It **transfers** across aerobic modalities, so other aerobic training holds it.
- **Peripheral (discipline-specific)** — sport-specific economy, muscle-recruitment pattern, and mechanical/tissue adaptations (bone loading, tendon stiffness). *Local.* It **does not transfer** — only that discipline builds and holds it.

**The rule (specificity):** *"Cross-training effects never exceed those induced by the sport-specific training mode."* So when an upkeep discipline slips, your other work holds the **engine**; it cannot hold the **specific part**. The nudge names both — honestly, gently, and per discipline.

## Per discipline — what holds, what slips, and the receipt

| Upkeep discipline slips | Engine held by | What does NOT transfer (the nudge) | Erosion speed | Receipt |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Run** | bike, swim | running economy + **bone-loading & tendon-stiffness** (impact tolerance) | moderate | bone/tendon adaptations are **mode-specific**; substituting cycling for running "trades biomechanical specificity for lower cumulative load" [4] |
| **Bike** | run, swim | cycling economy + sustained pedal power + the cycling muscular pattern | moderate | cross-training transfers the central engine but not sport-specific economy/power [1][5] |
| **Swim** | *almost nothing* | feel for the water, stroke mechanics, lat/upper-body endurance — highly technique-specific | **fast — the exception** | swimming shows **minimal VO2max transfer** to/from land sports; the aerobic engine barely cross-supports it [1][5] |
| **Strength** | *nothing endurance* | but strength is **durable on very low dose** | **slow — good news** | **1 session / 7 days maintained** strength, size & aerobic power; **1 / 14 days preserved 90–95%** over 12 wk [2]; ~6–10 sets/muscle/wk maintains [3] |

**Two facts fall out of the science, not from tuning to any one athlete:**
1. **Swim is the strongest nudge.** Cross-training does almost nothing for swim-specific fitness (technique + minimal aerobic transfer), so a swim-upkeep athlete who stops swimming loses it *fastest*. Say so plainly.
2. **Strength-upkeep is a *reassuring* nudge, not a warning.** The science says strength holds on surprisingly little — so the honest read is "no alarm, just don't drop to zero," not "you're slipping."

## How fast things fade (detraining timeline receipts)

- **Central (VO2max), fully stopped:** ~**7% decline in 12 days** of complete inactivity; larger over weeks (a master triathlete case: **9–11% over 12 weeks**) [5][6]. **Cross-training blunts this** — VO2max can be *fully maintained* by cycling in place of running over ≥4-week windows [1].
- **Peripheral (run economy, bone/tendon):** slower to lose but layoffs **beyond ~8–12 weeks** lose meaningful running-specific adaptation regardless of cross-training quality [4].
- **Strength:** the slowest to fade — held for **weeks** at 1 session/1–2 weeks [2].

So for a runner cross-training on bike/swim: the **engine holds** (weeks-to-months), the **legs' running-specific durability** is what quietly erodes. That is the true trade.

## The nudge — one shape, discipline-filled (gentle, science-true)

> *{Discipline}'s been under your upkeep target lately. {Other work} is holding your aerobic engine — but not {the discipline-specific thing}; that only comes from {discipline}. {gentle, science-true tail}.*

- **Run:** "…but not the running itself; economy and impact-tolerance only come from running. A little more run volume keeps it from slipping."
- **Bike:** "…but not the pedals; sustained power comes from riding. A ride or two holds it."
- **Swim:** "Nothing else really holds swimming — feel and stroke are swim-specific. This is the one to protect."
- **Strength:** "Strength holds on little — even one quality session a week keeps it. No alarm; just don't hit zero."

Tone is constant (credit the cross-training, name the specific gap, nudge without commanding). The **science** is per-discipline. Measure against the user's stored target in the discipline's own unit (e.g. run = `target_weekly_miles`), on the trailing pattern, not one partial week.
