# SCIENCE — Minimum Dose (strength maintenance / detraining avoidance)

**Status:** Citable grounding for the `minimum_dose` protocol (`minimum-dose.ts`). Like `SCIENCE-5x5-linear-progression.md`, this **separates peer-reviewed principle from program-design convention.** The protocol's *premise* — strength is retained on a reduced dose, and detraining is gradual — is **peer-reviewed and uncontroversial.** Its *exact dosing* is convention, and it carries **one honest tension worth flagging** (§3): the maintenance literature optimizes by keeping *intensity* high while cutting *volume*, whereas `minimum_dose` trades some intensity for minimal recovery cost.

**Why this doc:** the protocol cited **nothing** — no header references, no spec, no doc. The maintenance principle is real; this records it honestly and flags where the protocol's choices diverge from what the literature optimizes.

---

## 1. What it prescribes

A **strength-maintenance slot** (explicitly "won't build strength") for an athlete who can't/won't spend training budget on strength development but wants to avoid detraining:
- **1–3 sessions/week**: 1 full-body maintenance (required); +1 upper, +1 lower (optional, for 2–3×).
- Full-body maintenance: **2–3 × 6–8 reps @ ~60–73% 1RM**, mild Base/Speed progression, **RIR 3–4**; optional sessions lighter (~50% / bodyweight).
- **Intentionally no progression curve** — a holding pattern, not a gains stimulus.

## 2. The cited principle (peer-reviewed — SOUND)

- **Strength is maintained on a REDUCED dose.** Bickel, Cross & Bamman (2011) showed resistance-training adaptations are retained on roughly **1/3 of the training volume** (down to ~1 session/week) for weeks — *provided intensity is maintained* [Bickel 2011]. Spiering et al. (2021) reviewed the **minimal dose** to preserve strength and reached the same conclusion: you can cut volume/frequency substantially and still preserve strength [Spiering 2021].
- **Detraining is gradual for strength.** Mujika & Padilla (2000) — strength and neuromuscular adaptations decline **slowly** relative to endurance/aerobic markers; even small maintenance stimulus delays the loss [Mujika & Padilla 2000].

So the protocol's core premise — "a small dose keeps strength from sliding" — is **well-evidenced. Graded SOUND.**

## 3. What's convention (and the honest tension)

- **The exact dose** (2–3 × 6–8 @ 60–73%, RIR 3–4, the session structure) is practitioner programming — **convention, no per-figure source.**
- **The honest tension — flag it, don't hide it:** the maintenance literature is consistent that the variable you must *protect* is **intensity (load)** — you can drop volume hard, but strength maintenance depends on **continuing to lift heavy** [Bickel 2011; Spiering 2021]. `minimum_dose` deliberately uses **moderate intensity (~60–73% 1RM, RIR 3–4)** to minimize recovery cost. That is a **defensible time/recovery tradeoff** for an athlete spending their budget on endurance — but it means `minimum_dose` is a **conservative "won't lose much" hold, not the intensity-preserving maintenance the literature optimizes.** A strictly literature-optimal maintenance would keep loads higher and volume lower. The protocol's name is honest about this (it's a *minimum* dose, prioritizing cost over strength-optimality).

## 4. The honest grading

| Claim | Grade |
|---|---|
| Strength is retained on a reduced-volume dose | **SOUND** (Bickel 2011, Spiering 2021) |
| Detraining of strength is gradual | **SOUND** (Mujika & Padilla 2000) |
| The exact 2–3×6–8 @ 60–73%, RIR 3–4 dose | **CONVENTION** — no per-figure source |
| Moderate intensity (vs the literature's "keep load high") | **CONVENTION + flagged tension** — a recovery-cost tradeoff, not strength-optimal maintenance |

**Bottom line:** `minimum_dose`'s premise is **soundly grounded** (maintenance on a reduced dose is real). Its dosing is convention, and its one notable divergence — moderate intensity where the literature would keep load high — is a **deliberate minimal-cost tradeoff**, recorded here rather than dressed up as optimal. **Supplementary by design** (a maintenance slot inside an endurance week). *(Excluded from the runtime allow-list today; this doc grounds it for if/when it's surfaced.)*
