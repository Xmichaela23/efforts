# Efforts — Product Positioning

**Version:** 1.1 — 2026-05-27
**Author:** Michael Angel (solo founder)

---

## What Efforts Is

Efforts is the training app for endurance athletes who want to get stronger.

Not beginners. Not elite racers. The athlete in the middle — the runner who's been at it a few years and wants to finally add lifting without destroying their legs. The cyclist who rides four days a week and doesn't know where to put the gym. The triathlete who keeps getting injured because nothing is managing their total load.

These athletes are underserved by every existing tool. Endurance apps ignore the gym. Lifting apps ignore the ride schedule. The hybrid athlete is left to figure it out alone — and most of them get it wrong.

Efforts gets it right.

---

## The Core Problem

Endurance athletes who try to add strength training face three problems nobody solves for them:

**1. They don't know when to lift.** Squatting the day before a long run wrecks the run. Lifting after a threshold session means the weights suffer. The sequencing matters enormously and it's completely non-obvious.

**2. They don't know how hard to lift.** Endurance athletes who lift too heavy accumulate fatigue that tanks their training. Lifting too light wastes the session. The load has to be calibrated against what else is happening that week.

**3. They don't know what to lift.** The exercises that help a runner are different from the exercises that help a cyclist. Generic gym programs don't account for sport-specific needs, interference patterns, or the phase of training they're in.

Efforts solves all three. Automatically.

---

## What Efforts Does

Efforts reads your endurance training — from Garmin, from Strava, from what you tell it — and builds a strength program that fits around it. Not a generic lifting plan bolted onto your existing schedule. A strength protocol that knows:

- What sport you do
- What phase of training you're in
- How much fatigue you're carrying
- Which muscle groups you're already stressing from endurance work
- When to push and when to back off

When you log a long ride on Saturday, Efforts knows Sunday isn't the day for heavy squats. When you're two weeks out from a race, Efforts shifts to maintenance loads. When you're in base phase building aerobic volume, Efforts has you building strength. The two programs move together, not independently.

## Who It's For

**The athlete:** Intermediate endurance athletes — runners, cyclists, triathletes — who train consistently (3-6 days per week) and want to add strength training without sacrificing their endurance performance or getting injured.

**They've been doing their sport for at least a year.** They're not figuring out how to run or ride. They're figuring out how to get faster and stronger at the same time.

**They've tried lifting before** — or they know they should — but they don't know how to make it fit. They've either skipped the gym entirely, followed a generic program that left them too sore to train, or done a few random exercises without any real structure.

**They don't need a coach.** They're self-directed, tech-comfortable, and willing to follow a plan. They just need the plan to be smart enough to account for everything they're doing.

**They may or may not have a race goal.** Some are training for a specific event. Others just want to be fitter, stronger, and more durable. Both are valid. The race goal is optional — Efforts works either way.

---

## The Sports

Efforts supports any combination of endurance sport + strength:

- **Running + strength** — the most common hybrid athlete
- **Cycling + strength** — cyclist who wants to get stronger without losing watts
- **Triathlon + strength** — the full multi-sport hybrid
- **Cycling + running + strength** — duathlete or multisport athlete

Swimming is supported as part of triathlon. It is not a standalone offering today.

The strength component is always present. Efforts is not an endurance-only app. If an athlete doesn't want to lift, there are better tools for them.

---

## The Differentiator

**Every other app treats strength and endurance as separate programs.**

Garmin gives you training load for your runs and rides. It has no idea what you did in the gym.

TrainingPeaks builds you an endurance plan. Strength is a text note in the calendar.

Whoop tracks your recovery. It doesn't tell you what to do with it.

Strava is a social network with fitness features. It doesn't program you.

AI coaching apps (Future, Vi, etc.) give you a coach. They don't have the sport science to manage hybrid load automatically.

**Efforts is the only app where the endurance plan and the strength plan are built together, know about each other, and adjust together.**

The interference asymmetry between strength and endurance is built into the engine. The sequencing is automatic. The load is managed across both. This is not a feature — it is the product.

## The Deeper Mission

Efforts exists to keep endurance athletes athletic for the long haul.

Endurance training done in isolation chips away at muscle mass, bone density, and functional strength over time. The runner who never lifts eventually becomes the runner with the shuffle. The cyclist who never lifts eventually becomes the cyclist with the hunched shoulders, no power off the bike, nothing left when the road tilts up. It happens gradually, and by the time it's obvious it's hard to reverse.

This isn't about age. It's about training intelligence. Every mile logged without complementary strength work is a small withdrawal from a reserve most endurance athletes don't know they have. Efforts interrupts that pattern — not by turning endurance athletes into bodybuilders, but by giving them exactly the strength work they need to stay powerful, upright, and durable for as long as they want to keep moving.

The athlete who integrates strength early keeps it longer. The one who waits spends years trying to get it back. Efforts makes the integration automatic — so the default outcome for a consistent Efforts athlete is that they're still running or riding strong a decade from now, with the posture, power, and resilience that endurance training alone never builds.

That's the animal. Keep people from becoming the slouched runner, the hollowed-out cyclist, the triathlete who finishes races but can't open a jar. Build the complete athlete.

## What Efforts Is Not

- Not a beginner app. The athlete needs to know how to run, ride, or swim. Efforts programs the training, not the sport.
- Not a pure endurance app. Athletes who don't want to lift should use Garmin Coach, TrainingPeaks, or Zwift.
- Not a pure strength app. Athletes who don't do endurance should use Whoop + any lifting app.
- Not a coaching marketplace. There is no human coach. The intelligence is in the engine.
- Not a social network. There are no segments, kudos, or leaderboards.

## Race Goals

Efforts can train athletes for:

**Running:** 5K / 10K / half marathon / marathon
**Cycling:** gran fondo / century / criterium / time trial
**Triathlon:** sprint / Olympic / 70.3 / full Ironman
**Duathlon:** standard / long course
**No race:** general fitness and performance

Race goal is optional. When present, it anchors the periodization (base → build → race-specific → taper). When absent, the plan runs on a rolling structure focused on sustainable progression.

## Product Principles

**1. The hybrid is the product.** Strength and endurance are not two separate programs. They are one integrated plan. Every decision in the engine reflects this.

**2. Smart server, dumb client.** The server computes everything. The client renders what it receives. The athlete never has to configure the integration — it's automatic.

**3. Evidence-based, not opinion-based.** The interference asymmetry, the load management, the periodization — all grounded in sport science. When the research is clear, the engine follows it. When it's contested, the engine documents the tradeoff.

**4. Commercial grade from day one.** The app an intermediate athlete pays for and recommends to their training partner. Not a prototype. Not a beta. A product.

**5. The athlete's time is the constraint.** Every athlete has a weekly hours budget. Efforts works within it — never prescribing more than what fits, always optimizing what's possible given the constraint.

## What This Means for the Engine

The sport profile spec (`SPORT-PROFILE-SPEC.md`) follows directly from this positioning:

- Sport profile is Arc identity — the athlete declares what they do, Efforts handles the rest
- Race goal is optional — the engine runs on rolling progression when no race is declared
- Strength is always present — it is not an add-on, it is a core feature of every profile
- The wizard leads with "what do you do" not "what race are you training for"
- The LLM narrative understands the hybrid context — it never treats the workout in isolation from the full training picture

The architecture decisions (smart server, Arc as single source of truth, session_detail_v1 contract) all serve this positioning. They are not technical choices made in isolation — they are the product.
