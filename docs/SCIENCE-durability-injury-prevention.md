# SCIENCE — Durability (injury-prevention / tissue-tolerance strength)

**Status:** Citable grounding for the `durability` protocol (`foundation-durability.ts`, id `durability` — the run-overlay injury-prevention protocol; **not** the `triathlon` "Multi-Sport Durability" module, which is separate). Like `SCIENCE-5x5-linear-progression.md`, this **separates peer-reviewed principle from program-design convention** and grades each. The protocol's *mechanism* (strength training prevents injury, dose-dependently; eccentric/tempo loading builds tendon tolerance) is **peer-reviewed and strong**; its *exact dosing* (set/rep/tempo/RIR numbers) is practitioner convention, calibrated by the athlete's response — not a physiological constant.

**Why this doc:** the protocol cited Lauersen/Rio/Harøy **only in a code header** — no binding doc. This makes the evidence real and reviewable, and flags honestly where the numbers are convention.

---

## 1. What it prescribes

A **supplementary injury-prevention overlay** for runners (not a standalone strength developer; not a barbell %1RM program):
- **2 sessions/week** (Lower-Durability A + Upper-Posture); a 3rd adds Lower-Durability B.
- **No heavy barbell, no %1RM** — DB / bodyweight / band loading by descriptor.
- Movements: eccentric **calf raises** (3s lowering), **step-ups** (2-1-2 tempo), **Copenhagen planks** (short→long lever), **single-leg RDLs**, posterior-chain + posture work.
- **Progression by tier + RIR**, not load %: within Base, difficulty escalates bilateral → single-leg → loaded-single-leg; **target RIR drops 3 → 2** as Base progresses.
- The header's own framing: *"Durability ≠ maintenance — you must get stronger to get more resilient."*

## 2. The cited principle (peer-reviewed — SOUND)

These are real, verifiable, and directly support the protocol's design:

- **Strength training prevents injury, dose-dependently.** Lauersen et al. (2014) meta-analysis of RCTs found strength training reduced sports injuries to **<1/3** of control rates [Lauersen 2014]; Lauersen et al. (2018) showed the effect is **dose-dependent** — more strength training, fewer injuries [Lauersen 2018]. This is the protocol's headline claim and it is well-evidenced.
- **Eccentric / heavy-slow tempo builds tendon tolerance.** The Alfredson heavy-load **eccentric calf** protocol for Achilles tendinopathy [Alfredson 1998] and **Heavy-Slow-Resistance** (slow tempo under load) [Beyer 2015] are the evidence base for the 3s-eccentric calf/tempo prescription. Isometric/tendon-load analgesia [Rio 2015] supports tendon-directed loading.
- **Region-specific prevention exercises** the protocol uses are individually RCT-backed: **Copenhagen adduction** halves groin problems [Harøy 2019]; the **Nordic hamstring** family roughly halves hamstring-injury rates in meta-analysis [van Dyk 2019].

So the *exercise selection and the eccentric/tempo emphasis* map directly onto peer-reviewed prevention literature. **Graded SOUND.**

## 3. What's convention (NOT peer-reviewed — flag honestly)

The protocol's **specific numbers** are practitioner programming, not citable constants:
- The exact rep counts (calf raises ~15-20, step-ups ~8-15/leg, Copenhagen ~20-30s holds).
- The **3-second** eccentric as the precise cadence (HSR uses "slow," ~3-4s — the exact figure is a program choice).
- The **RIR 3→2** progression and the early/mid/late-Base tier cadence.
- The phase mapping (Base/Speed/Race-Prep → tier/RIR) is the engine's periodization convention.

None of these has a per-figure source, and none should claim one. They are **convention, calibrated by the athlete's response** (can they hold the longer-lever Copenhagen at RIR 2? progress the tier; if form breaks, hold). The *direction* (progressive overload toward harder variations, lower RIR) is principled; the *exact dose* is judgment.

## 4. The honest grading

| Claim | Grade |
|---|---|
| Strength training prevents injury, dose-dependently | **SOUND** (Lauersen 2014/2018, RCT meta-analyses) |
| Eccentric/heavy-slow loading builds tendon tolerance | **SOUND** (Alfredson 1998, Beyer 2015, Rio 2015) |
| Copenhagen (adductor) + Nordic (hamstring) prevention | **SOUND** (Harøy 2019, van Dyk 2019) |
| The exact sets/reps/tempo/RIR/tier cadence | **CONVENTION** — calibrated by response, no per-figure source |
| "Get more resilient by getting stronger" (overload, not maintenance) | **SOUND in principle** (dose-dependence) / **CONVENTION** in the specific overload curve |

**Bottom line:** durability is a **soundly-grounded protocol with convention dosing** — the injury-prevention mechanism and the exercise selection are peer-reviewed; the precise numbers are practitioner choices the retest/response calibrates. It is **supplementary by design** (an overlay inside a runner's week), not a standalone strength program.
